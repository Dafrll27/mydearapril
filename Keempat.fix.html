The Trade-Off in Human Relationships

We all acknowledge that every person has strengths and weaknesses. In every interaction, be it friendships, romantic relationships, or even parent-child relationships, there is something called a trade-off.

When interaction happens, we gain certain benefits but also accept certain costs. While we enjoy the benefits we receive, at the same time, we must also accept the drawbacks. On the other hand, we must also be ready to tolerate weaknesses, just as we hope our own flaws will be tolerated. This is often necessary to avoid conflicts.

In every moment when someone does something unpleasant to us, those of us who dislike conflict often search for reasons to justify why we should let it go. Tolerance is reasonable when it does not strip us of dignity or self-worth. As long as it strengthens relationships, it is meaningful. But when tolerance becomes an excuse to ignore emotional abuse or manipulation, it is no longer tolerance, it is betrayal of ourselves.

Thus, distance is not cruelty, but a critical step to save both sides: ourselves from destruction, and the other person from false comfort that enables destructive behavior.

Our willingness to tolerate too much, our hesitation to set clear boundaries, and our silence in moments when we should have spoken up, all of these were decisions we made.
